Every morning, my school bus drives past the reservation sign.

It says our nation's name.

Then we get to school, and suddenly it's like we disappear.

In history class, we spend three weeks learning about Native Americans. My teacher says, "They hunted buffalo."

Past tense.

I raise my hand.

"We're still here."

She smiles politely and keeps talking.

At lunch, another kid asks if I live in a teepee.

I've never even seen one.

My grandmother teaches me our language after dinner because the school doesn't.

She tells me every word is another thread tying us to the people who came before us.

When I answer attendance every morning, I say "here."

I wish the textbooks would say it too.
